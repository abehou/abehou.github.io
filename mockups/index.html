Three design directions for abehou.github.io (plain view)

Open each in a new tab. Terminal view is unchanged. Pick one, or tell me which parts of which to combine.

A

Working Paper

Serif · narrow column · arxiv preprint

Black-on-cream, single 640px column, 17px serif body. Pubs as hanging-indent citations grouped by theme. The vibe: if you printed the page, it looks like a preprint draft. Quiet, dense, unmistakably academic.

charter · dense · no decoration · least risky
 B

Lab Notebook

Dark mode · two-column · echoes the terminal

Mono sidebar with $ abehou@stanford prompt, pubs rendered as cards with theme tags. Threads the identity between terminal and plain views, so the toggle feels like two views of the same thing instead of two unrelated sites.

jetbrains mono · cards · identity-continuous · medium risk
 C

Essay

Prose-led · drop cap · gwern-style longform

Opens with a real sentence, not a resume bullet. Publications are embedded inside the narrative as pull-quotes — you read about a research thread, and the pub that backs it appears in the margin-ish. One minimal SVG illustration. The most opinionated, the most "you" if the prose lands.

source serif · drop cap · highest personality · highest risk
My honest read: B is the safest good answer — it makes the terminal↔plain toggle feel intentional instead of arbitrary. C is the best answer if the opening paragraph is actually you (which I drafted but you should rewrite). A is the default "don’t embarrass me" option — strong but forgettable.

None of these touch the terminal view. All three use your real pub data and the three-theme grouping we discussed. No framework, no build step, just HTML + CSS.
