Stanford CS · PhD, 2025— · working with Diyi Yang, Chris Manning, Dan Ho

I study how language models behave when there is no verifier — no unit test, no ground-truth label, no clean reward — and whether we can nevertheless get them to reason, collaborate with humans, and simulate the messy social systems they are deployed in. I care about non-verifiable domains, human–AI collaboration, and social simulation.

Before Stanford I worked with Ben Van Durme, Daniel Khashabi, and Tianxing He at Johns Hopkins. Most of what I’ve published so far lives at the intersection of legal reasoning, text generation, and agent-based modeling.
