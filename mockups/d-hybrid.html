Abe Bohan Hou

Stanford CS · PhD 2025— · working with Diyi Yang, Chris Manning, and Dan Ho

I study how language models behave when there is no verifier — no unit test, no ground-truth label, no clean reward — and whether we can still get them to reason, collaborate with humans, and simulate the social systems they are deployed in. I care about non-verifiable domains, human–AI collaboration, and social simulation.

At Stanford I work with Diyi Yang, Chris Manning, and Dan Ho. Before Stanford, I was at Johns Hopkins with Ben Van Durme, Daniel Khashabi, and Tianxing He, mostly on legal reasoning and text generation.
